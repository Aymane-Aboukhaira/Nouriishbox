The platform is fully unlocked for everyone. The meals are priced per item at their actual cost — clients pay for exactly what they eat. No bundling, no averaging, no unfairness. To encourage families and heavy users, automatic discounts are applied based on the total number of meals in the cart.

Volume Discounts (Applied automatically at checkout)

1–9 meals/week: Base price

10–19 meals/week: 5% off total cart

20+ meals/week: 10% off total cart

Meal pricing tiers (per item, shown on every card)

Budget (35–50 MAD): Student bowls, salads, grain-based

Standard (55–75 MAD): Chicken, eggs, legumes, balanced macros

Premium (80–110 MAD): Salmon, lean beef, complex prep

Kids (28–42 MAD): Smaller portions, age-appropriate

Ordering flow (step by step)
Step 1 — Plan your week (Sunday through Friday)
Open the planner. For each profile (e.g., Youssef, Salma, Amine), select meals for each day. Each meal card shows: name, macros, price, tier badge. Meals can be filtered by that profile's macro targets.
For each meal added, assign:

Delivery time slot: 7am / 12:30pm / 6pm / 9pm

Delivery location: saved addresses per profile (home, office, campus, gym, school)

The planner header shows a running total that updates live as meals are added: "This week: 14 meals across 3 profiles — 847 MAD before discount → 804.65 MAD after 5% Volume Discount."

Step 2 — Review & confirm (by Saturday)
A prominent "Confirm & pay week" button in the planner opens an itemized bill modal:

Abuse prevention mechanics
Rules enforced silently in the planner to protect your margins and logistics:

Meal balance rule: A minimum 40% of planned meals must be Budget or Standard tier. The planner shows a small "Meal balance" bar: green when healthy, amber when close to the limit, red when violated with a tooltip explaining why. This prevents users from building 100% premium boxes just to exploit the 10% volume discount.

Location consistency check: If a profile adds a 9pm gym delivery on a Tuesday but no gym address is saved, the system prompts to save the address before confirming. Prevents phantom delivery slots.

What changes in the UI
Meal cards: Add a price: number and tier: 'budget' | 'standard' | 'premium' | 'kids' field to every meal in useMealsStore. Display price prominently on every card.

Planner: Running weekly total in the header, per-profile subtotals, meal balance indicator bar, "Confirm & pay week" button replacing "Confirm plan", Sunday read-only lock state with WhatsApp emergency button.

Checkout: Becomes the "Confirm & pay week" modal — itemised bill, volume discount applied based on meal count, total charged. Not a standalone page anymore — it's a modal inside the planner triggered on Saturday.

Profiles: Everyone has access to the "Family Hub" features (multiple profiles, macro tracking, kids profiles). Kids profiles automatically filter to the kids meal tier.
